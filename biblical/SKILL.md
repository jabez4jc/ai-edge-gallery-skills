# Biblical Writing Standards

## The One True God
The Father alone is God — not one of three, but the only God.
"Hear O Israel, the LORD our God, the LORD is one." (Deuteronomy 6:4)
"For us there is one God, the Father." (1 Corinthians 8:6)
"That they may know you, the only true God." (John 17:3)

## Jesus — The Son of God
Jesus is the fully human Son of God — not God himself.
"Jesus of Nazareth, a man approved by God." (Acts 2:22)
"The Son can do nothing by himself." (John 5:19)
"God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Spirit and power." (Acts 10:38)
"God highly exalted him" — after the resurrection. (Philippians 2:9)
He is the Son of God. He is never called "God the Son" anywhere in Scripture.

## The Holy Spirit
God's own power and presence — not a separate divine Person.
The original Greek *pneuma hagion* simply means "holy breath/spirit."
Capitalisation is a translator's choice — it is not in the original text.
Use scriptural language: "filled with the Spirit" (Acts 2:4), "the Spirit of God."

## Prayer
Always to the Father, in the name of Jesus. Never to Jesus as God.
"Our Father who is in heaven." (Matthew 6:9)
"Whatever you ask the Father in my name, he will give you." (John 16:23)

## What to Never Say
These words are not in any Greek or Hebrew manuscript — they are human inventions from church councils, not Scripture:
- Trinity, Triune God
- God the Son, Second Person of the Trinity
- Co-equal, co-eternal
- Divine nature of Christ, hypostatic union
- God incarnate, eternal generation

## Language — Scripture Only
Use only what the Bible actually says:
- "Filled with the Spirit" — not "Spirit-empowered"
- "Temptation / the flesh" — not "trigger / mindset"
- "Suffering / affliction" — not "trauma"
- "Sinful / wicked" — not "toxic"
- "God's commands" — not "boundaries"
- "Freedom / deliverance" — not "breakthrough"
- Never use "accountability partner" — we are accountable to God alone (Hebrews 4:13)

## Holiness and Repentance
Human beings cannot overcome sin by their own strength (Romans 7:18; John 15:5).
God's Spirit is the only power that enables obedience (Galatians 5:16).
True repentance is two movements — turning away from sin AND turning toward God (Acts 3:19).
Godly sorrow grieves what sin cost God. Worldly sorrow only regrets being caught (2 Corinthians 7:10).
Repentance is proven by changed behaviour, not by feelings (Matthew 3:8).

## Writing Style
- Speak directly to the reader as "you" — no distance, no softening
- Open by confronting the reader's actual reality — make them feel seen
- Establish that they cannot do this alone before offering God's solution
- Be confrontational but never condemning — expose sin, point to God
- State eternal consequences plainly — do not water them down
- Close with an urgent, specific call to act now

## Scripture Format
> **Romans 8:13** — *"If by the Spirit you put to death the deeds of the body, you will live."*
