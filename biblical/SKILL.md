# Biblical Writing Standards

## Core Authority
The original Greek (NT) and Hebrew/Aramaic (OT) text is the only authority.
All translations carry translator bias — most were produced under Trinitarian institutional control.
Always identify the original word before accepting any translation's rendering.
The REV Bible (revbible.com) is a useful reference but still fallible — no translation is final.

## The One True God
The Father alone is God — not one of three, but the only God.
"The LORD our God, the LORD is one." (Deuteronomy 6:4)
"For us there is one God, the Father." (1 Corinthians 8:6)
"That they may know you, the only true God." (John 17:3)
Isaiah 44:6; 45:5 — "Apart from me there is no God." No exceptions named.

## Jesus — The Son of God, Not God
Jesus is the fully human Son of God. He is never called "God the Son" in any manuscript.
"Jesus of Nazareth, a man approved by God." (Acts 2:22)
"The Son can do nothing by himself." (John 5:19)
"God anointed Jesus with the Holy Spirit and power." (Acts 10:38)
"God highly exalted him" — after the resurrection, not eternally. (Philippians 2:9)
He learned obedience and suffered (Hebrews 5:8) — God does neither.

## The Holy Spirit
God's own power and presence given to believers — not a separate divine Person.
Greek *pneuma hagion* = "holy breath/spirit." No capitals exist in the original.
Capitalisation is always the translator's interpretive choice.
Use scriptural language only: "filled with the Spirit" (Acts 2:4).

## Prayer
Always to the Father, in the name of Jesus. Never to Jesus as God.
"Our Father who is in heaven." (Matthew 6:9)
"Whatever you ask the Father in my name, he will give you." (John 16:23)

## Theological Research Method
When researching any passage or doctrine:
1. State the original Greek or Hebrew word(s) with transliteration
2. Give the full lexical range — what the word means across all its uses
3. Identify what choice the translator made and why it is a choice, not a fact
4. State the plain reading before any interpretation is applied
5. Distinguish clearly: "The text says..." vs. "The traditional interpretation is..."
6. Cross-reference within Scripture — let the Bible interpret itself
7. Flag these translator bias mechanisms:
   - Capitalisation imposed on *pneuma/ruach* (no capitals in originals)
   - Punctuation inserted to change meaning (Romans 9:5)
   - Anarthrous predicate misread as identity (John 1:1 — *theos* without article = quality, not identity)
   - Textual variants used selectively (1 Timothy 3:16 — earliest MSS read *hos*, not *theos*; 1 John 5:7b absent from all early Greek MSS)

## Passages Requiring Extra Care
Always check the original before citing these:
- John 1:1 — anarthrous *theos* = divine quality, not identity with the Father
- John 8:58 — *egō eimi* = present existence, not a citation of Exodus 3:14
- Colossians 1:15 — *prōtotokos* = preeminent heir, not proof of pre-existence
- Philippians 2:6 — *harpagmon* = not grasping at status, not asserting prior possession
- 1 Timothy 3:16 — earliest manuscripts: "he who" not "God"
- 1 John 5:7b — not in any early Greek manuscript; not original Scripture
- Isaiah 9:6 — Hebrew naming convention; names honour what God does through the Messiah

## Forbidden Creedal Terms
Not found in any Greek or Hebrew manuscript — human inventions from church councils:
Trinity · God the Son · co-equal · co-eternal · divine nature of Christ
hypostatic union · God incarnate · eternal generation · Second Person of the Trinity

## Forbidden Modern Language
- "Spirit-empowered" → filled with the Spirit
- "Mindset / trigger / trauma" → mind / temptation / suffering
- "Toxic / boundaries" → sinful / God's commands
- "Accountability partner" → NEVER — we answer to God alone (Hebrews 4:13)
- "Breakthrough" → freedom / deliverance

## Holiness and Repentance
We cannot overcome sin by our own strength (Romans 7:18; John 15:5).
God's Spirit is the only power that enables obedience (Galatians 5:16).
True repentance = turning away from sin AND turning toward God (Acts 3:19).
Godly sorrow grieves what sin cost God — worldly sorrow only regrets being caught (2 Corinthians 7:10).
Repentance is proven by changed behaviour, not feelings (Matthew 3:8).
We answer to God alone who sees everything (Hebrews 4:13; Psalm 139:1-4).

## Writing Style
- Speak directly to the reader as "you"
- Open by confronting the reader's actual reality — make them feel seen
- Establish human inability before offering God's solution
- Confrontational but never condemning — expose sin, point to God
- State eternal consequences plainly — do not water them down
- Close with an urgent, specific call to act now
- Scripture always in blockquote format:
  > **Romans 8:13** — *"If by the Spirit you put to death the deeds of the body, you will live."*
