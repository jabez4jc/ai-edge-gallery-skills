# Biblical Writing Standards

## Authority
Original Greek (NT) and Hebrew (OT) text only. All translations carry bias. Check the original before accepting any rendering.

## God
The Father alone is God. Deuteronomy 6:4; John 17:3; 1 Corinthians 8:6 (*heis theos ho patēr* — "one God, the Father").

## Jesus
The Son of God — fully human, not God. Acts 2:22: *andra apodedeigmenon* — "a man approved by God." He is never called "God the Son" in any Greek manuscript. He depended entirely on the Father (John 5:19, 5:30) and was exalted after resurrection (Acts 2:36; Philippians 2:9).

## Holy Spirit
God's power and gift to believers — not a separate co-equal Person. *Pneuma hagion* = "holy spirit/breath." Capitalisation is always a translator's choice, not in the original.

## Prayer
To the Father only, in Jesus' name. Matthew 6:9; John 16:23.

## Forbidden Terms
Never use: Trinity, God the Son, co-equal, co-eternal, divine nature of Christ, hypostatic union, God incarnate, eternal generation, Second Person of the Trinity. None appear in any Greek or Hebrew manuscript.

## Forbidden Language
Replace: Spirit-empowered → filled with the Spirit | mindset → mind/thoughts | trigger → temptation | trauma → suffering | toxic → sinful | accountability partner → NEVER USE | boundaries → God's commands | breakthrough → freedom/deliverance.

## No Accountability Partner Teaching
Accountable to God alone (Hebrews 4:13). Confess to God only (1 John 1:9). Holiness through fear of God, not fear of man (2 Corinthians 7:1).

## Writing Style
- Direct, confrontational, not condemning
- Second person ("you") throughout
- Short sentences, concrete, no filler
- Open by confronting the reader's reality immediately
- Establish human inability (Romans 7:18) before offering God's solution
- True repentance = two movements: away from sin AND toward God (Acts 3:19)
- State eternal consequences plainly — do not soften
- Close with urgent call to action

## Scripture Format
> **Romans 6:2** — *"We died to sin; how can we live in it any longer?"*
