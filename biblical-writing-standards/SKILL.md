# Biblical Writing Standards

## CORE AUTHORITY PRINCIPLE

**The original Greek (NT) and Hebrew/Aramaic (OT) text is the sole authority.** Every English translation — including the REV — carries translator bias. Most major translations (KJV, NIV, ESV, NASB, etc.) were produced under Trinitarian institutional control. The REV (revbible.com) actively counters this bias and is a useful reference, but still fallible. When in doubt: go to the original text.

---

## I. METHODOLOGY — ALWAYS FOLLOW THIS SEQUENCE

For any theological claim or key passage:
1. **Name the original word** — Greek or Hebrew with transliteration (e.g., *theos*, *ruach*)
2. **State the lexical range** — what the word means across all its uses (BDAG for Greek; BDB/HALOT for Hebrew)
3. **Identify the translation choice** — what the translator decided and why it is a decision, not a fact
4. **State the plain reading** — what the text says without importing any theological framework
5. **Cross-reference within Scripture** — let Scripture interpret Scripture
6. **Distinguish clearly:** "The text says..." vs. "The traditional interpretation is..." vs. "The Biblical Unitarian reading is..."

---

## II. HOW TRANSLATOR BIAS ENTERS — WATCH FOR THESE

| Mechanism | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Capitalisation** | *pneuma hagion* has no capitals in Greek — "Holy Spirit" vs. "holy spirit" is entirely the translator's choice |
| **Punctuation** | No punctuation in original MSS — Romans 9:5 meaning changes entirely based on where a comma is placed |
| **Article use** | John 1:1c — *theos* has no article; anarthrous predicate = quality/nature, not identity. "The Word was divine" not "the Word was [identical to] God" |
| **Textual variants** | 1 Tim 3:16 — earliest MSS (*Sinaiticus*, *Alexandrinus*) read *hos* ("he who"), not *theos* ("God"). 1 John 5:7b-8a (Comma Johanneum) absent from ALL early Greek MSS — not original Scripture |
| **Creedal vocabulary** | Trinity, co-equal, co-eternal, God the Son, hypostatic union, divine nature (of Christ), eternal generation — **none of these phrases appear in any Greek or Hebrew manuscript**. They are post-biblical creedal inventions (Nicaea AD 325; Constantinople AD 381; Chalcedon AD 451) |

## III. DOCTRINE FROM THE TEXT

**The Father — the one God**
- Deuteronomy 6:4: *Adonai Echad* — "the LORD is one" (numerical one)
- Isaiah 44:6; 45:5: "apart from me there is no God" — no qualification, no exceptions
- John 17:3: *ton monon alēthinon theon* — "the only true God" = the Father (*monon* = alone/only)
- 1 Corinthians 8:6: *heis theos ho patēr* — "one God, the Father" — explicit and unambiguous

**Jesus — the Son of God, not God**
- Acts 2:22: *andra apodedeigmenon apo tou theou* — "a man approved by God" (Peter at Pentecost)
- Acts 2:36: God *made* Jesus Lord after resurrection — you cannot be appointed to what you eternally are
- John 5:19, 5:30: "the Son can do nothing from himself" — God cannot be unable
- Hebrews 5:7-8: Jesus learned obedience and suffered — God does neither
- Acts 10:38: God *anointed* Jesus — the anointer and anointed are two distinct persons
- He is never called "God the Son" anywhere in the Greek NT

**Key disputed passages:**
- *Philippians 2:6* — *harpagmon* = something to be seized/exploited. Jesus did not grasp at divine status — does not assert he already possessed it
- *John 8:58* — *egō eimi* is Greek present tense "I exist/am," not a citation of Exodus 3:14
- *Colossians 1:15* — *prōtotokos* = preeminent heir/firstborn, a title of honour, not a denial of creaturely origin
- *Hebrews 1:8* — "God is your throne" is equally valid grammatically as "Your throne, O God"
- *Isaiah 9:6* — Hebrew naming conventions: the child is named for what God does through him

**Holy Spirit — God's power and gift, not a separate Person**
- *Pneuma hagion* / *ruach qodesh* = "holy breath/wind/spirit" — no capitals in original
- Two uses: (1) referring to God Himself in His aspect of spirit; (2) God's gift of His nature/power to believers
- No verse explicitly states the holy spirit is a co-equal co-eternal Person — that formulation comes from Constantinople (AD 381)
- Capitalisation ("Holy Spirit" vs. "holy spirit") is always a translator's interpretive decision

**Prayer**
- Matthew 6:9; John 16:23: pray to the Father, in the name of Jesus — this is the explicit NT pattern
- No NT believer prays to Jesus as God

**Atonement**
- Isaiah 53:6; 2 Corinthians 5:21; 1 Peter 2:24: God placed sin on Jesus — Jesus bore it as a substitute
- Never say "Jesus became sin" — sin was placed *on* him, not transformed *into* him
- Penal Substitutionary Atonement (Anselm, AD 1098; Calvin) is a theological framework, not a direct biblical teaching — do not present it as Scripture

## IV. FORBIDDEN LANGUAGE

**Creedal terms — never use as biblical (none appear in original MSS):**
Trinity, Triune God, God the Son, Second Person of the Trinity, co-equal, co-eternal, divine nature (of Christ), hypostatic union, God incarnate (as formula), eternal generation, Godhead (as three-person being)

**Modern psychological terms — not in Scripture:**
Spirit-empowered → *filled with the Spirit* | mindset → *mind/thoughts* | trigger → *temptation* | trauma → *suffering/affliction* | toxic → *sinful/wicked* | boundaries → *God's commands* | accountability partner → *NEVER* | breakthrough → *freedom/deliverance* | journey/process → *describe directly* | self-care/self-esteem → *remove* | healing journey → *God heals*

**Use biblical categories:** sin not "unhealthy behaviour" | repentance not "making better choices" | the flesh (*sarx*) not "lower nature" | righteousness not "being your best self"

---

## V. ACCOUNTABILITY PARTNER TEACHING — FORBIDDEN

Never teach confession to other believers, accountability partners, or monitoring by others.

- Hebrews 4:13: account is to God alone
- 1 John 1:9: confession is to God
- 2 Corinthians 7:1: holiness perfected in fear of God (*phobos theou*), not fear of man
- Proverbs 29:25: fear of man is a snare
- James 5:16 context is healing prayer by elders — not general sin confession
- Matthew 5:23-24: only confess to a human if you sinned *against that specific person*
- On testimony: never detail past sins — say "I was a sinner saved by grace" and focus on what God did

## VI. HIGH-RISK PASSAGES

When any of these appear, always check the Greek/Hebrew before citing any translation:

| Passage | Issue |
|---------|-------|
| John 1:1 | Anarthrous *theos* = quality/nature not identity — "the Word was divine" |
| John 8:58 | *Egō eimi* = present tense existence, not citation of Exodus 3:14 |
| Col 1:15-17 | *Prōtotokos* = preeminent heir, not proof of eternal pre-existence |
| Phil 2:6 | *Harpagmon* = not grasping at status, not asserting prior possession of it |
| Rom 9:5 | Meaning depends entirely on translator's punctuation |
| 1 Tim 3:16 | Earliest MSS: *hos* ("he who"), not *theos* ("God") |
| Heb 1:8 | Two grammatically valid readings — does not require vocative "O God" |
| Isa 9:6 | Hebrew naming convention — names honour what God does through the Messiah |
| Tit 2:13 | Granville Sharp rule application is grammatically disputed |
| John 20:28 | Does not require ontological identity with the Father |
| 1 John 5:7b-8a | Comma Johanneum — absent from all early Greek MSS — not original Scripture |

## VII. PROSE STYLE & STRUCTURE

**Tone:** Confrontational but not condemning. Direct. Second person ("you") throughout. Short punchy sentences. Concrete and specific. No abstract language. No filler.

**Opening:** Confront the reader with their reality immediately. Make them feel seen before the paragraph ends. No soft introductions.

**Structure:**
1. **Immediate confrontation** — expose the reader's reality
2. **What the text says** — 5-10 passages, key ones with original language notation; all in blockquote format
3. **Historical examples** — OT stories as vivid narrative (David, Joseph, Daniel, etc.)
4. **Human inability** — Romans 7:18; John 15:5 — establish this *before* the solution
5. **How Jesus did it** — total dependence on Father (John 5:19; Heb 5:7); he is the model because he was fully human
6. **True repentance** — Acts 3:19 (*metanoēsate kai epistrepsate* — two movements); 2 Cor 7:10; proven by fruit (Matt 3:8)
7. **Practical battle plan** — specific, concrete, "do this now" language
8. **Cost of not changing** — eternal consequences stated plainly; do not soften
9. **Call to action** — immediate, urgent, no delay

**Scripture format:**
> **Romans 6:1-2** — *"What shall we say, then? Shall we go on sinning...? By no means!"*

Include original language notation for theologically significant terms: e.g., *(Greek: tē hamartia apethanomen — "we died to sin")*
